REFLECTION

Thien-an Truong

When I started college, I was unsure about what I wanted to pursue. I thought it was computer science, but I quickly learned that it wasn’t for me.
I eventually self-taught myself to edit at a decent level and decided I wanted to try out digital storytelling.
I’ve learned alot about my video production, editing, photography, and digital storytelling overall throughout my years at college so far -- and I am continuing to learn and grow as I continue to pursue a bachelors in digital storytelling.

My interest in directing and editing started in middle school when I would I take a film class with some friends.
I enjoyed the creativity and the possibilities that making films had. We didn’t learn much about editing, besides combing clips, but I would go home and try to learn new and creative ways to make my videos stand out. After that class, I never really went back to filming or editing and kind of forgot about how much fun I had until college.

I started to edit in college again when I started getting really good at video games. I would have clips of my impressive plays in different video games and I would edit montages of them to different songs.
I had so much fun relearning how to edit and it was something I got really passionate about which led me to change my major during my sophomore year.

My goal in the future is to work on music videos. Two of my passions, music and film combined.
It might be ambitious, but I would like to work my way up until I am able to work with mainstream artists - but that is a very long way to go as I am still learning and getting the hang of things.
